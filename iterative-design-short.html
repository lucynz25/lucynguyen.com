The Project


Alongside a small team, I helped design an interactive interface based on the problem that the emerging startup has. Re-mint was a startup that we worked with, an official secondhand marketplace for fashion brands. We went through the full process of mocking up a solution to the startup’s concept: (1) sketching ideas of the interface, (2) creating an interactive, high-fidelity prototype, (3) conducting user testing on a final, revised prototype, and (4) contacting the start-up.



Project Scope


My team had 2 weeks to learn Figma, select a startup to come up with design solutions for, and go through the design process to create a prototype for them. We chose Re-mint: Re-mint lets fashion brands set up official secondhand marketplaces so their customers can buy and sell directly with each other. The startup help brands monetize their resale market. Re-mint offers revenue share on transaction fees and uses the transaction data to drive new sales. How the interface of the startup will be a space for all brands, sellers, and customers to interact with one another is a point of difference that we haven’t seen from other companies and intrigued us to concoct an interface for their particular needs.



Question

How can we solve the issues that Re-mint is currently dealing with?


PROCESS

Problems


  1. Lack of personalization. On current brand websites, there is little direct interaction with customers during the shopping process to guide them to their ideal products.
  2. Lack of brand presence in the resale market. On current secondhand sites, brands aren’t tied to sellers and don’t rely on sellers to market their items.
  3. Lack of user engagement. On current brand websites, there is little contact with customers after they purchase their products.

Our GOAL is to curate a mockup that solves these 3 problems, providing all types of users a common space to navigate the different features of the website easily and efficiently.


PROCESS

Design Approach


  1. Project Ideation: We initiated the project to search startups that we were interested in designing for.
  2. Rapid Prototyping: We initiated the design process by creating rapid prototypes which were rapidly sketching ideas for the interface.
  3. Wireframing: The design process involved the creation of wireframes to visualize the layout and structure of the interface.
  4. Low-Fidelity Prototypes: Low-fidelity prototypes were developed to provide a basic representation of the interface. Each group member contributed to generating different possible designs.
  5. Mid-Fidelity Prototypes: After iterations and feedback, the low-fidelity prototypes were refined to create mid-fidelity wireframes.
  6. High-Fidelity Prototypes: The project progressed to the creation of high-fidelity prototypes using Figma with iterations and critiques to refine the design.
  7. User Testing: User testing was conducted using the interactive high-fidelity prototype. Users were provided with tasks and questions to navigate the interface.
  8. Feedback Implementation: Feedback from users was incorporated into the design to enhance the interface. Changes were made based on the users' experiences.
  9. Final Prototype: A final version of the interactive prototype was developed.
  10. Additional Ideas: The team brainstormed additional ideas for the Re-mint interface which included online contests, a point rewards system, and outfit suggestions.
  11. Contacting Start-up: Finally, the refined prototype was sent to the company.

Rectrospective


What I would do differently next time?
  1. Iteration is a long but important process. With each feedback my team receieved, we went through iterations of refining the design of our interface. I didn't realized the importance of it till the end of our project, but iterating helped with finding out the small but key issues that would be otherwise missed.
  2. Communication is the key to a great design. Through this project, I realized the importance of communicating with your team members. Communicating with your team drastically prevents repeated decision making & misunderstandings and helps with expanding on each other ideas. Without my teammates, I wouldn't have improved in some areas of my communication (i.e not feeling like I'm a burden to my teammates when I ask clarifying questions).
