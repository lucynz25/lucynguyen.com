About Autinerary


Autinerary is a non-profit organization that supports autistic individuals, their siblings, their parents, and anyone within the autism community.

The goal is to be a central information hub for anything autism-related. Their mission revolves around answering the fundamental questions that families often face as they enter the world of autism so Autinerary aims for their website to serve as a comprehensive resource for them throughout their entire autism journey.



The Project


This project aimed to address a pressing issue of the lack of support and connectivity of siblings of individuals on the autism spectrum. The project's scope encompassed the creation of an online forum platform called Autinerary's Community where anyone, including siblings of those on the autism spectrum could connect, share experiences, and access valuable resources. This platform was designed to offer siblings a safe and welcoming space, fostering empathy and understanding among its users. Our ultimate and initial goal was to provide siblings with a platform where they could access information, advice, and emotional support from peers who shared similar experiences.

As the project evolved, it organically expanded its reach to include a broader community. In addition to the initial focus on siblings, we recognized the importance of including autistic individuals themselves, their family members, and anyone in the autism community to be able to use the discussion-forum platform. This expansion allowed us to create a discussion forum platform and support network that welcomed a diverse range of users, fostering a more comprehensive approach to promoting empathy, understanding, and support for everyone connected to autism.



Project Scope


In just 8 weeks, we developed a discussion forum platform, a cornerstone product for Autinerary. Our project unfolded week by week, with evolving responsibilities for each team member, myself included as a designer.



PROCESS

My Role


As the sole leading product designer on the team, I oversaw all design aspects of the project. This consists of conducting user interviews, implementing UX research, creating wireframes & obtaining feedback, and then developing prototypes. I was responsible for addressing all design-related queries and decisions to ensure a discussion-forum platform that offers a seamless user experience for our target audience: siblings of individuals with autism and individuals in the autism community.



PROCESS

Project Approach


Designing the discussion forum platform was a multifaceted project that required a comprehensive and user-centric approach to meet its goals. Below, I outlined the key phases and steps in the process:

  1. Initiation: We initiated the project with a clear mission to create a discussion forum platform for siblings of individuals on the autism spectrum within Autinerary.
  2. User Insights: User interviews provided deep insights into the needs of siblings of individuals with autism, informing design approach.
  3. Competitive Analysis: I studied existing forum platforms to identify unique value propositions and differentiate Autinerary's Community.
  4. User Flows: I created user flows to visualize and helped optimize the platform's navigation.
  5. Wireframes: Early wireframes were developed and iterated upon, enhancing the platform's structure and design.
  6. Interactive Prototypes: Prototypes allowed users and stakeholders to experience the platform's functionality before development.
  7. Developer Collaboration: I closely communicated with the development team to ensure the design vision was realized effectively.
  8. User Testing and Optimization: Continuous user testing and feedback informed design revisions for the product.
  9. Final Outcome: The project culminated in the launch of a vibrant and user-friendly platform for siblings of individuals with autism, fostering community and connections.


PROCESS

User Insights


Given the timeline of the project and our specific target users, we were only able to conduct 3+ user interviews. These interviews played a pivotal role in gaining profound insights into the unique needs and challenges faced by siblings of individuals with autism. Working within a tight timeframe, we synthesized these insights to craft 2 detailed personas. These personas, stemming from the firsthand experiences shared during interviews, now help formulate feature and design ideas for the Autinerary's Community platform.

  • The platform should integrate links or designated spaces for resources, support groups, or dedicated spaces for positive conversations.
  • A clean, intuitive, and user-friendly interface is essential. Clear navigation, easy accessibility, and a responsive design will enhance the overall user experience, making the platform easy to use to a diverse user base.
  • Employing a color palette designed to invoke calmness will create a welcoming atmosphere for all users, promoting inclusivity and comfort within the platform.
  • The platform must actively discourage discriminatory practices by implementing a robust community guidelines section.
  • The platform should be designed to accommodate a wide range of experiences and perspectives.

PROCESS

User Flows


I crafted user flows to visually map the navigation of the platform. These five user flows specifically focused on guiding users to find essential links on the homepage, including the FAQ, community rules, Autinerary website, profile, and post creation. By concentrating on these key interactions, the user flows aims to enhance the overall navigability of the platform's homepage, streamlining the process for users to access crucial information and functionalities.



PROCESS

Wireframes


With the sitemap finalized, I moved onto wireframing. All of the designs were done on Figma. Given the focus on building the minimum viable product (MVP) for Autinerary's Community platform, I concentrated on designing the features for only laptop screen resolutions.

Throughout this process, I generated over 10+ low-fidelity wireframes. Within the timely development of the product, I prioritized and selected specific features to be included in the high-fidelity wireframes ensuring a more streamlined approach for developers. The emphasis was on optimizing the platform for an efficient user experience while delivering essential product functionalities within the project's scope.

Usability Testing


I conducted three usability tests but I wasn't able to incorporate the user feedback into my designs given the time constraints of the project. In summation, these are the three key areas of revisions that users suggested in regards to the Autinerary's Community platform.

  • Users recommended the inclusion of educational and resourceful content; having a resources section will help fulfill this.
  • Users recommended adding instructions on how to use the discussion forum platform.
  • Users recommended implementing moderation tools to uphold the safety values promised by Autinerary.

While design changes weren't integrated, these insights provided a roadmap for refining the platform post-launch and ensuring a more robust user experience. These recommendations will be handed off to the designer tasked with refining the product and implementing additional features post-launch.

Rectrospective


What I would do differently next time?
  1. Learning how to balance multiple tasks is critical for staying on track and meeting deadlines effectively. This project was exceptionally fast-paced, making it challenging to ensure that I delivered my best when faced with numerous tasks. I plan to work on my time management skills and employ tools like Trello to efficiently manage multiple tasks, enabling me to navigate tight deadlines more effectively in future projects.
  2. Always apply responsive redesign. In this project, I neglected to consider how my illustrations would translate to a mobile interface. In future projects, I will place a strong emphasis on responsive design, ensuring that my designs adapt seamlessly to a variety of screen sizes.
  3. Remember, the user always comes first. Designing in a short timeframe with a high workload can lead to neglecting the core purpose of the project which is serving the user. In future projects, I will ensure in every step that I prioritized the user's needs and experience.
