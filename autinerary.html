The Project


Our project aimed to address a pressing issue in the support and connectivity of siblings of individuals on the autism spectrum. The project's scope encompassed the creation of an online forum platform where anyone, including siblings of those on the autism spectrum could connect, share experiences, and access valuable resources. This platform was designed to offer siblings a safe and welcoming space, fostering empathy and understanding among its users. Our ultimate goal was to provide siblings with a platform where they could access information, advice, and emotional support from peers who shared similar experiences.


Project Scope


In just 8 weeks, we developed a discussion forum platform, a cornerstone product for Autinerary. Autinerary, a central hub for autism-related information. Autinerary offers resources, support, and knowledge for individuals and families navigating the complex world of autism. Our project unfolded week by week, with evolving responsibilities for each team member, myself included as a designer.


PROCESS

My Role


As the sole leading product designer on the team, I oversaw all design aspects of the project. This included conducting user interviews, implementing UX research, creating wireframes & obtaining feedback, and then developing prototypes. I was responsible for addressing all design-related queries and decisions to ensure a seamless user experience for our target audience: siblings of individuals with autism and overall discussion-forum users.


PROCESS

Project Approach


Designing the discussion forum platform was a multifaceted project that required a comprehensive and user-centric approach to meet its goals. Below, I outlined the key phases and steps in the process:

  1. Initiation: We initiated the project with a clear mission to create a discussion forum platform for siblings of individuals on the autism spectrum within Autinerary.
  2. User Insights: User interviews provided deep insights into the needs of siblings of individuals with autism, informing design approach.
  3. Competitive Analysis: I studied existing forum platforms to identify unique value propositions and differentiate SibChat.
  4. Streamlined User Flows: I created user flows to visualize and helped optimize the platform's navigation.
  5. Wireframes: Early wireframes were developed and iterated upon, enhancing the platform's structure and design.
  6. Interactive Prototypes: Prototypes allowed testing users and stakeholders to experience the platform's functionality before development.
  7. Developer Collaboration: I closely communication with the development team to ensured the design vision was realized effectively.
  8. User Testing and Optimization: Continuous user testing and feedback informed design enhancements.
  9. Final Outcome: The project culminated in the launch of a vibrant and user-friendly platform for siblings of individuals with autism, fostering community and connections.

Rectrospective


What I would do differently next time?
  1. Learning how to balance multiple tasks is critical for staying on track and meeting deadlines effectively. During the SHC project, This project was exceptionally fast-paced, making it challenging to ensure that I delivered my best when faced with numerous tasks. I plan to work on my time management skills and employ tools like Trello to efficiently manage multiple tasks, enabling me to navigate tight deadlines more effectively in future projects.
  2. Always apply responsive redesign. In this project, I neglected to consider how my illustrations would translate to a mobile interface. In future projects, I will place a strong emphasis on responsive design, making certain that my designs adapt seamlessly to a variety of screen sizes.
  3. Remember, the user always comes first. Designing in a short timeframe with a high workload can lead to neglecting the core purpose of the project, which is serving the user. In future projects, I will ensure in every step that I prioritized the user's needs and experience.
