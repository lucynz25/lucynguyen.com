About Solutions in Hometown Connections


Solutions in Hometown Connections (SHC) is a non-profit organization that supports vulnerable refugee and immigrant populations in Maryland, Virginia, and Washington, DC.

The goal is to help families who are struggling navigate their new home. There are many programs and resources that SHC offers. They include but are not limited to: Back to School Supply Drive/Fundraisers, Citizenship and Naturalization Guidance, Early Childhood and Youth Help, English Classes, and Welcome Workshops.



The Project


SHC’s website is the primary portal for visitors to obtain information about the programs and resources offered.

SHC wants to increase requests for support and volunteers through their website, ultimately helping them achieve their mission of building better communities for vulnerable refugee and immigrant populations.

Currently, SHC's website faces challenges with volunteer engagement, donor outreach, and program accessibility.

Improving the user experience and streamlining messaging through a website redesign will make it easier for families to find information and access the support they need as well as for volunteers and potential donors to learn about SHC's programs and ways they can support the organization.

Importantly, SHC's primary objective is to enhance engagement with donors and volunteers through their website, as these key stakeholders play a pivotal role in providing help to SHC in order for them to help families in vulnerable communities. Their is more of a prioritization in improving the user experience of volunteers and donors, than families.



Project Scope


Throughout the 16 weeks, we were working towards the goal of a fully functional, responsive Squarespace website providing a seamless, engaging, and informative experience for all users. The website will serve as a bridge between SHC and its community, connecting volunteers, donors, and beneficiaries to the organization's mission and impact. It will contribute significantly to SHC's goal of building stronger communities for their target populations.



Question


How can the redesign of the Solutions in Hometown Connections (SHC) website enhance volunteer and donor engagement while improving program accessibility and the user experience for families seeking support?



PROCESS

My Role


As a designer, I enhanced the usability and overall user experience of the Solutions in Hometown Connections website, effectively highlighting the organization's wide range of programs and services. Collaborating with fellow designers in the team, we began by conducting interviews and in-depth UX research, and iteratively crafting wireframes for the website. Through an ongoing feedback loop involving the design manager, product manager, and clients, we refined our designs eventually implementing them on the SquareSpace platform. Every element of the website including information architecture, layout, and creative illustrations underwent a complete overhaul.



PROCESS

Project Approach


The redesign of Solutions in Hometown Connections' website was a multifaceted project that required a comprehensive and collaborative approach to meet its goals. Below, I outlined the key phases and steps in the process:

  1. Initiation: We began the project in summer '23 with a clear mission to enhance the website for Solutions in Hometown Connections.
  2. Research: We connected with the client and conducted interviews to deeply understand SHC and its users.
  3. Competitive Analysis: We studied similar non-profit websites to identify best practices.
  4. Affinity Diagramming: We used affinity diagramming to organize our user interview findings in order to help formulate design ideas for the redesigning of the website.
  5. Sitemap and Architecture: We restructured the website for intuitive navigation.
  6. Design: We created wireframes and revamped the site's layout for a user-friendly experience.
  7. Development: We implemented the design using Squarespace and introduced custom features.
  8. User Engagement: We optimized the site to connect with volunteers, donors, and beneficiaries.
  9. Final Outcome: The project concluded with a fully functional, responsive Squarespace website, aligning with SHC's mission to support vulnerable populations.

    • PROCESS

    Research: Interviews


    To infuse Solutions in Hometown Connections' website redesign with authentic perspectives, we engaged in insightful interviews with a diverse range of stakeholders. Our conversations with dedicated volunteers, generous donors, and resilient refugee and immigrant participants were pivotal in shaping the project's direction.

    These interviews allowed us to tap into the real-life experiences and challenges faced by those directly impacted by SHC's initiatives. By listening to their stories, we gained a deep understanding of the organization's impact and the nuances of the support it provides. These firsthand narratives enriched our design choices, ensuring that the website would resonate with its intended users on a personal level.

    Through these interactions, we discovered valuable insights that guided our decision-making process, from creating user-friendly navigation to showcasing success stories. By integrating the voices of those who matter most, we ensured that the redesigned website would be a true reflection of the organization's mission and its significance to the lives it touches.



    PROCESS

    Competitive Analysis


    By evaluating the websites of other non-profits serving refugee and immigrant populations, we were able to pinpoint strengths and weaknesses in their user experiences. This process provided invaluable insights into how to best showcase SHC's unique programs, resonate with their target audience, and make critical information easily accessible.

    PROCESS

    Sitemap and Information Architecture


    Before starting on the low-fidelity wireframes, we rearranged the informational structure of the website in order to make navigation intuitive and efficient. After getting feedback from our clients, you can see our improved IA below.

    The main changes to the sitemap include the following:

    • Reducing numerous pages/sub-pages within navigation bar dropdown.
    • Creating flow of information with sections within pages.
    • Adding informative sections like “Our Impact,” “Our Reach,” and “Stories” to give credibility of programs.



    PROCESS

    Design


    With the sitemap finalized, we moved onto sketches and wireframes. All of the designs were done on Figma and Squarespace.


    Due to time constraints, the high-fidelity wireframes were streamlined without detailed color schemes and specific nuances typically associated with comprehensive design. Our priority was to promptly restructure and implement the design on Squarespace to identify any potential challenges or limitations, allowing us to iterate and devise solutions effectively.

    Limitations with Squarespace



    Throughout the redesign journey of the Solutions in Hometown Connections website, we encountered a series of challenges stemming from the limitations of the Squarespace platform. These hurdles, while initially daunting, provided us with the opportunity to exercise creativity and innovation in problem-solving.

    By identifying workarounds, utilizing custom CSS, and leveraging the platform's strengths, we were able to transform limitations into opportunities. The final result showcases a website that not only meets the organization's objectives but also overcomes technical restrictions to deliver a seamless and engaging user experience.

    Rectrospective


    What I would do differently next time?
    1. Don't be afraid to ask questions. During the SHC project, I had the chance to expand my knowledge by learning from my design manager. Her insights into auto layout and design industry standards were invaluable. In the future, I'll actively reach out to my mentors and managers for design questions and learning oppportunties.
    2. Communication is key. The SHC project highlighted the significance of clear and consistent communication with the client and team members, especially in the context of remote collaboration. In upcoming projects, I will continue to uphold this standard of communication to ensure that everyone remains informed which will enabled us to push forward in achieving project goals.
